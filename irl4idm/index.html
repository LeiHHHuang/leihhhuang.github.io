Overview

Learning inverse dynamics without action labels

Abstract

Video generation models offer a scalable way to plan robot behavior from broad physical priors, but turning generated videos into executable actions remains difficult. IRL4IDM learns this inverse dynamics mapping from the same action-free videos used to train or finetune the video generator.

The method first learns a dense reward by temporally aligning unlabeled demonstrations, then trains a goal-conditioned policy with pseudo-subgoals sampled from those videos, and finally finetunes the policy on model-generated subgoals to reduce domain shift.

Why it matters

  • Removes dependence on action-labeled robot demonstrations.
  • Avoids handcrafted grasping heuristics used by prior methods.
  • Improves precisely where baselines tend to break: nuanced manipulation.

Method

A three-stage training pipeline

01

Reward learning via temporal alignment

A visual encoder is pretrained with temporal cycle-consistency, producing an embedding space where progress toward subgoals can be measured densely.

02

Pseudo-subgoal RL for inverse dynamics

Uniformly sampled frames from demonstrations act as intermediate goals, enabling online reinforcement learning without random exploration alone.

03

Finetuning on generated subgoals

The policy is adapted to the distribution of model-generated plans, reducing the gap between demonstration frames and synthesized future observations.

Finetuning matters

Figure 5

Replacing pseudo-subgoals with model-generated subgoals during the final stage consistently improves performance, with gains ranging from 12 to 28 absolute points.

Design takeaways

Ablations
  • Milestone rewards substantially improve optimization over dense reward alone.
  • Sparse environment success provides useful additional supervision.
  • A slightly looser subgoal-switching threshold can help when the learned encoder is imperfect.
