IRL4IDM grounds synthesized visual subgoals into executable robot actions without action-labeled demonstrations or handcrafted grasping heuristics.

Abstract

Video generation models are emerging as scalable robot planners, but grounding generated videos into executable robot actions remains challenging. IRL4IDM learns an inverse dynamics model from the same action-free video demonstrations used to train or finetune video generation models, without relying on action labels or heuristic grasping primitives.

Our approach first learns a dense reward from temporally aligned videos, then trains a goal-conditioned inverse dynamics policy with pseudo-subgoals sampled from demonstrations, and finally finetunes the policy on model-generated subgoals to reduce domain shift.

Method

IRL4IDM follows a three-stage pipeline: reward learning from unlabeled videos, pseudo-subgoal-conditioned inverse dynamics learning, and finetuning on model-generated subgoals.

01. Reward Learning

A visual encoder is pretrained with temporal cycle-consistency, producing dense progress signals directly from action-free videos.

02. Inverse Dynamics via RL

Uniformly sampled pseudo-subgoals from demonstrations guide online reinforcement learning of a goal-conditioned inverse dynamics policy.

03. Finetuning with Generated Subgoals

The policy is further adapted to the distribution of synthesized goals to reduce mismatch between demo frames and generated plans.

Evaluations

IRL4IDM is evaluated on Meta-World and LIBERO, showing strong gains on both grasping and non-grasping tasks, especially in settings that require nuanced interaction.

Meta-World Overall 84.0%

Best overall result across 5 evaluated tasks.

LIBERO Overall 84.0%

Large gain over prior video-grounding baselines.

Finetuning Gain +12 to +28 pts

Consistent improvement from generated-subgoal finetuning.

Meta-World

IRL4IDM achieves 56.0% on hammer and 80.0% on assembly, two tasks that prior methods struggle with.

LIBERO Kitchen Scene

IRL4IDM achieves 88.0% on opening the microwave, 96.0% on opening the drawer, 84.0% on placing the bowl on the plate, and 68.0% on placing the bowl on top of the cabinet.

Finetuning Matters

Key Takeaways

  • Milestone rewards substantially improve learning stability.
  • Sparse environment success gives useful additional supervision.
  • A slightly looser subgoal threshold can improve training when reward embeddings are imperfect.
